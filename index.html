Main Types of Music

Music can be broadly categorized into two

  • popular music.
  • classical music.

Popular music is widely listened to and tends to be more commercial, while classical music is more complex and formal.

  1. POPULAR GENRE OF MUSIC

    1. Pop

      2. Pop music is short for popular music and is a genre of popular music that originated in its modern form in the United States and United Kingdom during the mid-20th century.
      It is produced for mass consumption and is typically characterized by:
      • simple melodies,
      • catchy rhythms,
      • danceable beats.

    2. Rock

      3. Rock music is a broad genre of popular music that originated as "rock and roll" in the United States in the late 1940s and early 1950s, developing into a range of different styles in the mid-1960s and later, particularly in the United States and the United Kingdom.
      It has its roots in 1940s and 1950s rock and roll, a style that drew heavily on African-American genres such as blues and rhythm and blues, and from country music.

    3. Hip Hop

      4. Hip hop music is a genre of popular music developed in the United States by inner-city African Americans and Latino Americans in the Bronx borough of New York City in the 1970s.
      It consists of a stylized rhythmic music(rap) that commonly accompanies:
      • rapping,
      • a rhythmic speech and
      • rhyming speech that is chanted
      .

    4. Electronic

      5. Electronic music is music that employs electronic musical instruments, digital instruments, and circuitry-based music technology in its creation. It is all about creating sound using technology.

    5. R&B and Soul

      6. Rhythm and blues (R&B) and soul are genres of popular African-American music that originated in the United States in the 1940s and 1950s.
      They combine elements of jazz, blues, and gospel music. Soul music emerged from rhythm and blues in the 1950s and early 1960s with a greater emphasis on vocalists, often featuring soulful singing characterized by melisma and vocal improvisation.

  2. CLASSICAL GENRE OF MUSIC

Classical music is a broad term that refers to the formal written tradition of Western music, including a vast repertoire of music from the Renaissance period to the present day. It is characterized by its:
  • complexity,
  • structure,
    • and
  • use of notation.
These are just a few of the many different types of music that exist in the world. With so many genres to choose from, there is sure to be a type of music that everyone enjoys.
